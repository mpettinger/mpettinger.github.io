Business Analysis

Flight School Aquisition | Saint Louis, MO

June 2015
  • Acquisition Strategy & Financial Modeling: Authored a comprehensive 44-page acquisition business plan for a $140,000 buyout of a flight training provider in St. Louis. Developed multi-year pro-forma financial statements, including Income Statements, Balance Sheets, and Cash Flow projections using both cash and accrual accounting methods.
  • Cost-Benefit & Break-Even Analysis: Conducted a granular cost-of-goods-sold (COGS) analysis, accounting for fuel volatility (100LL), engine overhaul reserves, and mandatory 100-hour inspections. Identified a break-even sales target of approximately $106,912 following a proposed fleet modernization.
  • Predictive Analytics & Market Research: Performed a deep-dive market analysis using FAA Airmen Statistics to identify a specific growth opportunity in the Light Sport Aircraft (LSA) segment. Forecasted an industry growth rate of 6.6% per year for Sport Pilots—a projection that aligned with actual market CAGR of 5-7% over the following decade.
  • Competitive Intelligence & Gap Analysis: Executed a SWOT analysis of five regional competitors, identifying a critical service gap in LSA training accessibility. Proposed a strategic asset replacement (Cessna 152 to Kitfox S-LSA) to lower the barrier to entry for the 13.3% of surveyed leads who "didn't know where to start".
  • Process Improvement: Designed a student recognition and retention framework—leveraging digital milestones and challenge coins—to address the industry-wide challenge of high student dropout rates following solo flight.
